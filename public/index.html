🔒 How Hashing Works

When you type or pick your birthday, your browser runs it through a hash function (SHA-256). This creates a unique "fingerprint" (the hash) which is sent to the server. Your actual birthday never leaves your device.

🐄 Think of it like turning a cow into a burger: easy to go from cow → burger, but impossible to reconstruct the cow from the burger. It's a one-way mathematical function.

Security Note: Birthdays are weak passwords because an attacker can just try all possible dates (~36,500), hash them, and see which one matches yours.
