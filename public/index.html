📊 Why Does Password Type Matter?

Two passwords can produce completely different-looking SHA-256 hashes, yet one can be cracked in milliseconds and the other would take millions of years. The difference is not the hash algorithm — it is the size of the search space.

🔍 How the cracker works:
For a birthday there are only ~27,000 plausible dates. The cracker hashes every single one in under 40 ms and finds a match instantly. For an 8-character alphanumeric password there are 218 trillion possibilities — the same approach would take years.

🔒 How Hashing Works

      

        When you type or pick your birthday, your browser runs it through a hash function (SHA-256).
        This creates a unique fingerprint which is sent to the server.
        Your actual birthday never leaves your device.
      

      

        🐄 The Cow to Burger Analogy:

        Think of it like turning a cow into a burger. It is easy to go from cow → burger,
        but absolutely impossible to reconstruct the original cow from the burger.
        Hashing is a one-way mathematical function designed exactly like this.
      

      

        Your password is never sent to the server. Instead, your browser computes this one-way fingerprint — and only the hash is transmitted.
      

    


    

      ⚠ The Illusion of Safety:

      Even though we use a secure SHA-256 hash (meaning the server never sees your password), this system is still extremely easy to hack.
      Why? Because birthdays are predictable.
    


    

      An attacker doesn't need to "reverse" the hash. They just use a brute-force function:
      they take every possible date in a 100-year range (~36,500 dates), hash each one, and compare them to your hash.
      On a modern computer, this takes less than a second.
      This project proves that even with strong math (hashing), a weak or predictable password makes your security irrelevant.

💡 Key Takeaways

      
    
        
  • Birthdays are trivial to crack — under 40 milliseconds, even in a browser
    • 
        
  • Length beats complexity — 12-char alphanumeric (102M years) >> 8-char + special chars (193 years)
    • 
        
  • GPUs obliterate weak passwords — what takes 6.9 years in a browser falls to 2.8 hours on a $1,600 GPU
    • 
        
  • This assumes NO salting — with proper salting, GPU attacks become impractical even for weak passwords
